Assignment:
Properly apply design patterns in the design and implementation of an information system.

Animal: Each animal must be assigned to the correct pavilion specialised for a particular species.
Each animal must be registered in the application.

Pavilion : Each pavilion has its own capacity and types of animals for which it is dedicated.

Visitor : Visitors can view the pavilions and animals via the web. They can also buy a tick online.
Visitors can become a sponsor of the zoo.

Zoo Keeper : Each zoo keeper is only allowed to feed certain species or animals. Some zoo keepers may feed animals within multiple pavilions.

Sponsor : The sponsor can financially support the selected animal, the entire pavilion or provide a financial donation and the zoo will then decide how to use the donation.




Zoo cash desk
Within the zoo, design a cash register information system that allows the zoo to:
sell tickets on a cash register (on-site)
sell tickets online.
Cash register information system also supports:

tariffs for various groups of customers (children, adults, students, ... )
integration with turnstiles
various reporting views
Apply at least 5 different design patterns
Apply at least 5 different design patterns when designing a zoo cash register system. At least three must be applied to address business requirements. The others can be applied to address the technical parts.
Outputs
High level design of the system
Detailed design of particular parts of the system in which the chosen design patterns were applied
Brief introduction of the applied patterns
Explanation of the pattern usage

Within the use of the pattern, use pseudo-code to capture how you would use the pattern. You will not be required to functionally solve or program the entire application.
